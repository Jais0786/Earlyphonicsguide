Common Phonics Mistakes Parents Make

When children begin learning to read, many well-intentioned practices can unintentionally make reading harder.

1. Starting with Too Many Letters

Introducing a large number of letters at once can overwhelm young learners. Starting with a small group of sounds helps children focus on the logic of blending.

2. Rushing Into Reading Words

Some children need more listening and sound awareness practice before blending letters into words. Skipping this step often leads to frustration.

3. Encouraging Guessing

When children guess words based on pictures instead of sounds, decoding skills may develop more slowly. True reading relies on sound structure, not memorization.

4. Practicing for Too Long

Short, structured practice sessions usually work much better than long, unstructured lessons for preschool children.
